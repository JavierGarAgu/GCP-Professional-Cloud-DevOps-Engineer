# Incident Communication - SRE Practice

## Question Summary

During a large customer-facing incident, there is no estimated time for resolution. Internal stakeholders and customers are requesting updates. The goal is to communicate efficiently with all affected people.

## Correct Answer

**B - Provide periodic updates to all stakeholders in a timely manner. Commit to a "next update" time in all communications.**

## Explanation

In SRE incident management, communication is a key part of the response process. Even if there is no ETA for the resolution, stakeholders need regular and predictable updates.

The communication team should provide updates to both internal teams and customers. Each message should include when the next update will be available, reducing uncertainty and avoiding repeated requests for information.

The goal is not only to solve the technical problem, but also to maintain transparency and trust during the incident.

## Why Other Options Are Incorrect

- **A:** Only focuses on internal stakeholders. Customer-facing incidents require communication with all affected users.
- **C:** Responding only to customers does not provide balanced communication. Internal stakeholders also need updates.
- **D:** The Incident Commander should focus on coordinating the technical response, not managing all communications.

## SRE Principle

During incidents, communicate frequently, clearly, and consistently. Regular updates are important even when there is no new information.
